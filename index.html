üí∞ What is Profit First?

Profit First is a cash management system created by Mike Michalowicz. The core idea is simple: instead of the traditional formula (Revenue - Expenses = Profit), you flip it:

Revenue - Profit = Expenses

By taking profit FIRST, you force your business to operate on what remains. This ensures you're always profitable, always paying yourself, and always saving for taxes.

ü™£ The Four Buckets

üéØ

Profit (5-20%)

Your reward for being in business. This is NOT for reinvesting ‚Äî it's for YOU. Take a quarterly "profit distribution" to celebrate your success. This account should feel like a vault you rarely touch.

üë§

Owner's Pay (30-50%)

Your salary. This is what you live on. It should be consistent and predictable, just like a "real job." Pay yourself on a regular schedule (bi-weekly or monthly).

üèõÔ∏è

Tax Reserve (10-15%)

Set aside for quarterly estimated taxes. Having this money ready means no scrambling in April. In Oregon, plan for federal + state + self-employment (roughly 25-35% of profit depending on your bracket).

‚öôÔ∏è

Operating Expenses (30-50%)

Everything else: rent, software, contractors, marketing, supplies. This is the ONLY money available to run your business. If you can't afford something with OpEx, you can't afford it.

üí° Pro Tips

  • Open separate bank accounts. Literally have 4 accounts (Profit, Owner's Pay, Tax, OpEx). Seeing the money separated makes the system work.
  • Allocate on the 10th and 25th. Twice a month, move money from your main account into the buckets. Consistency is key.
  • Start with where you ARE, not the targets. If you're currently at 0% profit, don't jump to 5%. Start at 1% and increase 1% each quarter.
  • The Profit account is a vault. Only touch it quarterly for profit distributions. Make it hard to access (different bank, no debit card).
  • OpEx should feel tight. That's the point. Constraints force you to cut waste and get creative.
  • Pay yourself a real salary. Stop taking random "owner's draws." Set a consistent bi-weekly or monthly pay amount from the Owner's Pay account.
